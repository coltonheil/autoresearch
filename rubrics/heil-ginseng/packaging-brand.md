# Packaging Brand Direction Rubric — v1.0

## Purpose

Score brand direction concepts for Heil Ginseng packaging on a 1–10 scale per dimension (6 dimensions). Used to evaluate 24–30 directional concepts in the Phase 0.4 autoresearch loop before narrowing to top 5 finalists. Concepts are Brand Direction Briefs (not finished designs), so scoring focuses on strategic positioning, buyer resonance, and feasibility — not pixel-level execution.

This rubric has **two hard gates** that override the weighted score:
- **BTD Citation Gate**
- **Cultural Depth Gate**

If either gate FAILs for a concept, the concept is **excluded from scoring aggregation** regardless of its composite score.

---

## Hard Gates (Override Weighted Score)

### 1. BTD Citation Gate — PASS / FAIL

Question: does every persona evaluation cite at least 1 specific BTD entry?

**PASS when:**
- Every persona scoring this concept cites ≥1 `[BTD-XXX]` reference in their evaluation
- Cited BTD entries are relevant to the dimension being scored (not decorative)
- At least 90% of dimension scores across all personas have supporting BTD citations

**FAIL when:**
- Any persona evaluation has zero BTD citations
- Citations are irrelevant to the dimension being scored (e.g., citing Amazon pricing data for Cultural Appropriateness without connecting it to buyer behavior)
- More than 30% of dimension scores lack supporting citations

**Operational rule:** If a concept fails the BTD Citation Gate, it is marked `[EXCLUDED-NO-CITE]` and removed from scoring aggregation. The raw scores are still recorded for transparency but carry zero weight in ranking.

### 2. Cultural Depth Gate — PASS / FAIL

Question: does the Chinese Diaspora persona evaluation cite ≥5 distinct cultural data points for this concept?

**PASS when:**
- Chinese Diaspora persona references ≥5 distinct cultural observations from BTD or competitive audit
- References span multiple cultural dimensions (gifting norms, TCM context, Didao, color symbolism, number symbolism, root morphology, etc.)
- At least 2 references are specific to Chinese buyer behavior, not generic "wellness" data

**FAIL when:**
- Chinese Diaspora persona cites fewer than 5 cultural data points
- Cultural references are all from the same dimension (e.g., only color symbolism, only gifting)
- No references are specific to Chinese buyer segments from BTD

**Operational rule:** If a concept fails the Cultural Depth Gate, the Chinese Diaspora persona's scores for that concept are flagged `[LOW-CULTURAL-DEPTH]` and de-weighted by 50% in aggregation.

## Scoring Dimensions

Total composite: weighted average across 6 dimensions, each scored 1–10.

| Dimension | Weight | What It Measures |
|-----------|--------|------------------|
| Premium Positioning Credibility | 20% | Does this direction credibly read as premium wellness, not commodity supplement? |
| Buyer Segment Resonance | 25% | Does it resonate across multiple real buyer segments (grounded in BTD)? |
| Competitive Differentiation | 15% | Does it occupy whitespace identified in the competitive audit? |
| Cross-Channel Versatility | 10% | Works on Amazon thumbnail, Meta ad, Shopify PDP, trade show booth, gift box? |
| Cultural Appropriateness | 15% | Sensitive to Chinese diaspora gifting norms, TCM context, no appropriation? |
| Implementation Feasibility | 15% | Achievable within budget and timeline from cost-benefit analysis? |

**Weights sum: 100%**

## Dimension Definitions

### 1. Premium Positioning Credibility (20%)

Assess whether this direction supports premium pricing ($25–80+ per product) and reads as premium wellness, not commodity supplement.

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 9–10 | Unmistakably premium. Direction supports pricing at the top of the category. Visual language, positioning, and tone all communicate quality-first. Could sit alongside Ritual, David Protein, or Fly By Jing in a premium wellness editorial. |
| 7–8 | Strongly premium. Direction supports premium pricing for most SKUs. One or two elements may read slightly mid-market but the overall impression is aspirational. |
| 5–6 | Adequately premium. Direction supports mid-premium positioning. May read as "nice supplement" rather than "premium wellness brand." Risk of blending with mid-tier competitors. |
| 3–4 | Weakly premium. Direction reads as commodity or generic. Pricing ceiling limited by brand perception. Difficult to justify premium pricing. |
| 1–2 | Anti-premium. Direction actively undermines premium positioning. Reads as budget, mass-market, or clinical. |

### 2. Buyer Segment Resonance (25%)

Assess how well this direction speaks to real buyer segments documented in the BTD. Must be grounded in data, not assumption.

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 9–10 | Resonates strongly with 4+ buyer segments. Direction has clear appeal hooks for Traditional Health, Premium Gift, Active Wellness, and Chinese Diaspora buyers. Each resonance claim cites specific BTD data. |
| 7–8 | Resonates with 3+ segments strongly, 1–2 segments moderately. Clear primary segment fit with secondary appeal. Most resonance claims cite BTD data. |
| 5–6 | Resonates with 2 segments. One-dimensional appeal. May work for one channel (e.g., Amazon) but creates friction for others (e.g., Chinese gift market). |
| 3–4 | Narrow appeal to 1 segment. Direction serves one buyer type at the expense of all others. Cross-segment friction is significant. |
| 1–2 | No clear segment resonance. Direction is generic or misaligned with documented buyer motivations. |

### 3. Competitive Differentiation (15%)

Assess whether this direction occupies whitespace from the competitive audit's positioning map and shelf analysis.

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 9–10 | Occupies clear, unoccupied whitespace. Direction creates immediate visual/positioning separation from Zen, Dairyland, Hsu's, Prince of Peace, and Burmeister. Would stand out on Amazon shelf in 3-second test. |
| 7–8 | Strong differentiation from most competitors. May share one attribute with a competitor but overall positioning is distinct. Above-average shelf stand-out. |
| 5–6 | Moderate differentiation. Shares some visual/positioning territory with 1–2 competitors but has one distinguishing element. Average shelf presence. |
| 3–4 | Weak differentiation. Direction clusters with existing competitors on positioning map. Would be hard to distinguish in Amazon grid. |
| 1–2 | No differentiation. Direction is visually/positioning identical to an existing competitor or falls into a crowded quadrant. |

### 4. Cross-Channel Versatility (10%)

Assess whether this direction works across all required sales channels.

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 9–10 | Works flawlessly across all channels: Amazon thumbnail (3-second scan), Meta ad (scroll-stopping), Shopify PDP (premium detail), trade show booth (large format), gift box (tactile premium). Scales up and down without losing identity. |
| 7–8 | Works well on 4 of 5 channels. One channel may require adaptation but core identity survives. Amazon and Meta are strong. |
| 5–6 | Works on 3 of 5 channels. Significant adaptation needed for 2 channels. May be strong digitally but weak physically, or vice versa. |
| 3–4 | Works on 1–2 channels only. Direction is channel-specific and doesn't translate. |
| 1–2 | Channel-incompatible. Direction fundamentally doesn't work for the primary sales channels. |

### 5. Cultural Appropriateness (15%)

Assess sensitivity to Chinese diaspora gifting norms, TCM context, and cross-cultural respect. No cultural appropriation or insensitivity.

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 9–10 | Deeply culturally informed. Direction demonstrates nuanced understanding of gifting norms (red/gold, auspicious numbers, no sets of 4), TCM context (Yin-nourishing, cooling properties, Didao provenance), and Chinese buyer expectations. Chinese Diaspora persona can cite ≥5 cultural data points. Direction could be marketed in Chinatown without embarrassment. |
| 7–8 | Culturally respectful and mostly informed. Understands major cultural norms. May have minor gaps in TCM nuance but nothing offensive or tone-deaf. Chinese Diaspora persona cites ≥5 data points. |
| 5–6 | Culturally neutral. Direction doesn't violate major norms but doesn't demonstrate cultural depth either. Generic wellness positioning that happens to be inoffensive. Chinese Diaspora persona cites 3–4 data points. |
| 3–4 | Culturally superficial or mildly tone-deaf. Uses Chinese cultural elements without depth (e.g., cherry blossoms, generic "Eastern" motifs). May inadvertently violate a norm (e.g., white packaging for gift market). |
| 1–2 | Culturally insensitive or appropriative. Direction uses sacred cultural symbols carelessly, ignores documented buyer preferences, or positions Chinese culture as exotic decoration rather than authentic heritage. |

### 6. Implementation Feasibility (15%)

Assess achievability within budget and timeline from the Phase 0.3 cost-benefit analysis.

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 9–10 | Fully achievable. Direction aligns with Option A or low-end Option B cost structure. No new infrastructure, vendors, or capabilities needed. Launches within current timeline. |
| 7–8 | Achievable with minor adjustments. Falls within recommended Option B budget. May require one new vendor or capability but nothing exotic. Manageable timeline impact. |
| 5–6 | Moderately feasible. Falls at the high end of Option B or low end of Option C budget. Requires 2–3 new capabilities or vendor relationships. Timeline impact is significant but manageable. |
| 3–4 | Challenging. Requires Option C-level investment. Multiple new capabilities, significant vendor coordination, or extended timeline. Risk of scope creep. |
| 1–2 | Not feasible at current scale. Direction requires investment, capabilities, or timeline that is unrealistic for Heil Ginseng's current operation. |

## Keep / Discard Threshold

| Decision | Condition |
|---|---|
| **KEEP** | Composite ≥ 6.5 AND BTD Citation Gate PASS AND Cultural Depth Gate PASS |
| **DISCARD** | Composite < 6.5 OR either hard gate FAIL |
| **Marginal flag** | Composite 6.5–7.0 — keep but deprioritize in ranking |
| **Top candidate** | Composite ≥ 8.0 — advance to adversarial challenge round |

## Anti-Patterns (Penalize in Scoring)

- **Generic wellness positioning:** "natural," "clean," "holistic" without specificity. Scores ≤5 on Buyer Segment Resonance.
- **Option B gravitational pull:** Safe, middle-ground evolution that pleases no one strongly. Look for direction that lacks strong scores AND weak scores — mediocrity across the board.
- **Cultural insensitivity:** White/black packaging for Chinese gift market, sets of 4, inauspicious numbers, decorative use of Chinese characters without meaning.
- **Ignoring mobile/shelf:** Direction that only works on a desktop website or in print. Amazon thumbnail and Meta mobile feed are the primary proving grounds.
- **Clinical supplement aesthetic:** Lab coats, white backgrounds, scientific notation. This is not the Heil brand.
- **Stock photo heritage:** Generic farm photos, tractors, amber grain fields. If it could be any Wisconsin farm, it's not differentiated.
- **Naming that loses "Heil":** Any Option C name that doesn't preserve a connection to the family name or Wisconsin provenance loses existing (however small) brand equity.

---

## Rewarded Patterns (Give Bonus Within Ranges)

- **Data-grounded positioning:** Every strength claim cites a specific BTD entry or competitive audit finding.
- **Whitespace occupation:** Direction lands in the unoccupied Premium + Modern quadrant on the positioning map.
- **Cross-segment appeal:** Strong scores from 3+ personas, not just one.
- **Cultural depth without appropriation:** Uses authentic TCM knowledge and Wisconsin provenance (both real) rather than borrowed aesthetic motifs.
- **Shelf-differentiation:** Direction would visually stand out in an Amazon search grid alongside Zen, Dairyland, Hsu's.
- **Gift-worthiness:** Direction that works for Chinese gifting context AND Western premium gifting.
- **Budget-conscious creativity:** High-scoring direction that achieves premium feel within Option A or B budget.

## Scoring Protocol

For each concept × persona × dimension:

1. Persona reads the Brand Direction Brief
2. Persona identifies ≥1 BTD citation that informs their evaluation
3. Persona scores dimension 1–10 with 1-sentence justification citing the BTD entry
4. Persona assigns confidence level (High / Medium / Low) based on data density for that persona × dimension intersection
5. Scores without BTD citations are auto-flagged `[UNSUPPORTED]`
6. Confidence levels are recorded for weighted aggregation:
   - High confidence: full weight
   - Medium confidence: 85% weight
   - Low confidence: 70% weight, flagged `[LOW-DATA]`

### Aggregation

- Persona-weighted composite = Σ(persona_weight × dimension_weight × score × confidence_multiplier)
- Concepts with >30% `[UNSUPPORTED]` scores are flagged for review
- Concepts failing either hard gate are excluded from ranking (scores recorded but zero-ranked)
