# Ad Creative Copy Rubric — v1.0

## Purpose

Score 0–100 for any Meta Ads copy variant produced in the ad creative loop. Scored after the visual passes the visual rubric. Decides whether a complete image+copy package is kept or discarded.

---

## Scoring Dimensions

Total: 100 points across 5 dimensions.

| Dimension | Weight | Points |
|---|---|---|
| Hook Strength | 30% | 0–30 |
| Benefit Clarity | 25% | 0–25 |
| CTA Effectiveness | 20% | 0–20 |
| Brand Voice | 15% | 0–15 |
| Compliance | 10% | 0–10 |

**Weights sum: 100%**

---

## Dimension Definitions

### 1. Hook Strength (30 pts)

The hook is the first 1–2 lines. It must earn the scroll-stop alone.

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 27–30 | Immediately arrests attention. Specific, intriguing, or emotionally resonant. Does not rely on generic wellness language. Would stop someone mid-scroll without image support alone. Speaks to a real, felt motivation. |
| 21–26 | Solid hook. Clear and on-point but not exceptional. Engages someone already warm to ginseng/wellness. |
| 14–20 | Weak hook. Functional but forgettable. Could be written for any supplement. |
| 7–13 | Generic opener. Starts with "Discover," "Try," "Feel the difference," or similar low-energy openers. |
| 0–6 | No hook. Opens with brand name, product name, or a dry statement. Loses the scroll immediately. |

Hook format guidance:
- Ideal length: ≤125 characters for first sentence (shows above "See more" on mobile)
- Pattern options: provocative question, unexpected specific fact, bold direct claim (within compliance), vivid sensory frame
- Farm-heritage specifics score higher than vague wellness generalities

### 2. Benefit Clarity (25 pts)

Assesses whether the copy clearly communicates what the customer gets and why it matters to them.

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 22–25 | Primary benefit is unmistakable on first read. Specific, credible, and tied to a real customer motivation. Secondary benefit adds rather than dilutes. No benefit cannibalism (two benefits that undercut each other). |
| 17–21 | Benefit is clear but could be sharper or more specific. Slightly vague — e.g., "natural energy" without context. |
| 11–16 | Benefit mentioned but buried or generic. Customer must work to find the value proposition. |
| 5–10 | Multiple benefits listed with no priority. Feels like a feature dump, not a benefit story. |
| 0–4 | No clear benefit communicated. Copy describes the product without saying what it does for the buyer. |

Benefit specificity tiers (high → low):
1. Specific outcome tied to real life: "The energy that powers a 4 AM farm shift. Bottled."
2. Named benefit: "Supports natural energy and mental clarity."
3. Category: "Good for energy."
4. Non-benefit: "Made with ginseng root."

### 3. CTA Effectiveness (20 pts)

The call to action must close the gap between interest and click.

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 18–20 | Clear, direct, low-friction CTA. Tells the customer exactly what to do and what they get. Matches the offer and the ad's emotional energy. |
| 14–17 | Good CTA but slightly mismatched to offer, or slightly too generic (e.g., "Learn more" when "Shop now" is available). |
| 9–13 | Weak CTA. Vague direction. "Visit our website" or similar non-specific language. |
| 4–8 | Buried or confusing CTA. Customer has to search for what action to take. |
| 0–3 | No CTA. Copy ends without a direction. |

CTA format guidance:
- First-choice: "Shop now → heilginseng.com" or "Get yours → free shipping over $30"
- Second-choice: "Try it today" + URL
- Avoid: "Click the link in bio" (not applicable to Meta ads), "Learn more" without specificity

### 4. Brand Voice (15 pts)

Assesses alignment with Heil Ginseng's voice: honest, farm-grounded, confident without hype, heritage-rooted.

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 13–15 | Sounds unmistakably like Heil Ginseng. Warm, direct, farm-honest. "Texas-sharp" in clarity — no fluff, no hype, no generic wellness corporate speak. Heritage, craft, and trust come through without being forced. |
| 10–12 | Mostly on-voice. One phrase or sentence sounds slightly generic or borrowed from a broader wellness brand. |
| 6–9 | Partially on-voice. Feels like a wellness brand but not specifically Heil Ginseng. Could be for any supplement. |
| 3–5 | Off-brand. Hype-y, salesy, or clinical. Doesn't feel authentic. |
| 0–2 | Wrong brand entirely. Sounds like a pharma ad, a generic detox brand, or an influencer promotion. |

Voice guardrails:
- Avoid: "Transform your life," "Unlock your potential," "Feel the power of nature"
- Avoid: excessive exclamation points, emoji-heavy copy in primary text
- Prefer: specificity over enthusiasm, farm story over lab story, confidence over hype
- Heil Ginseng voice references: 4th-generation farm, Marathon County Wisconsin, Joe Heil, "real roots"

### 5. Compliance (10 pts)

Assesses FDA/FTC supplement advertising rule adherence. This is a gate dimension — hard fails trigger immediate discard.

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 9–10 | Fully compliant. No disease claims, no prohibited language, any health claims are appropriately hedged ("may support," "traditionally used for"). |
| 7–8 | Mostly compliant with one soft flag — e.g., an overused phrase ("boosts immunity") that Ben should review but is not automatically prohibited. |
| 4–6 | Multiple soft flags or one moderate risk. Requires significant Ben review and likely rewrite. |
| 1–3 | Contains high-risk language. Copy references specific conditions or makes unsubstantiated comparative claims. |
| 0 | **Hard fail.** Contains prohibited claims. See hard discard rules below. |

---

## Hard Discard Rules (Auto-0 on Compliance Dimension)

Any of these triggers immediate DISCARD of the copy variant. Log the specific violation.

| Violation | Examples |
|---|---|
| Disease claims | "cures diabetes," "treats anxiety," "prevents cancer," "heals joints" |
| Condition references | Any named medical condition presented as a target outcome |
| "Clinically proven" without citation | Unsubstantiated clinical efficacy language |
| Deceptive urgency/scarcity | Fabricated countdown timers, false stock claims |
| FTC testimonial violations | Implied endorsements without disclosure, results presented as typical when atypical |
| Misleading comparatives | "Better than prescription," "More effective than any supplement on the market" |

---

## Keep / Discard Threshold

| Decision | Condition |
|---|---|
| **KEEP package** | Copy score ≥ 65 AND compliance hard fail = none |
| **DISCARD copy** | Copy score < 65 OR compliance hard fail triggered |
| **Regen allowed** | Up to 2 copy regenerations for the same image before image is also discarded |
| **Marginal flag** | Score 65–69 — keep but deprioritize in ranking |
| **Improvement gate** | New copy scores ≥ 2 pts higher than previous kept copy on same brief/angle — worthwhile; otherwise note as marginal |

---

## Anti-Patterns Reference

Penalize these across dimensions, especially Hook Strength, Brand Voice, and Compliance:

- **Clickbait hooks**: sensational, misleading, or exaggerated opener that the body copy can't substantiate
- **Medical claims**: any language that implies treatment, diagnosis, cure, or prevention of a disease
- **Generic supplement language**: "packed with antioxidants," "nature's superfood," "the power of plants"
- **Hype overload**: multiple exclamation points, ALL CAPS emphasis, "AMAZING results"
- **Vague CTAs**: "Click here," "Visit our site," "Learn more" without context
- **Feature dumping**: listing product specifications without translating them to customer benefits
- **Corporate wellness speak**: "holistic wellness journey," "elevate your lifestyle," "transform your health"

---

## Rewarded Patterns

Give bonus consideration (within scoring ranges) for:

- **Farm-specific specificity**: Marathon County, Joe Heil, 4th-generation, Wisconsin — earns trust immediately
- **Tactile/sensory language**: language that makes the customer feel or taste something real
- **Confident understatement**: doesn't over-promise; lets quality speak — scores higher on Brand Voice
- **Unexpected specificity in the hook**: a fact, number, or frame the customer hasn't heard before
- **Compliance-safe benefit language**: "may support," "traditionally used," "supports natural" — clean framing

## Copy Length Guidelines

| Placement | Primary Text | Headline | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| Feed — Standard | 90–150 chars (above fold) | 27 chars max | 27 chars max |
| Feed — Expanded | Up to 400 chars total | — | — |
| Stories | 90 chars or less | — | — |

Score copy assuming the truncated view. Hook must work alone in 125 chars.
